1. What is SmartPM?

SmartPM is an app that helps you manage construction projects. It keeps track of your money, materials, work tasks, and team. It works with another app called SmartPlans. SmartPlans creates your project estimate. SmartPM takes that estimate and helps you run the job from start to finish.

Think of SmartPM as your project binder. Everything you need is in one place: your budget, your schedule, your daily notes, and your paperwork.

2. Getting Started

What is this?

This section shows you how to open SmartPM and log in.

How to Log In

  1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Edge, etc.)
  2. Go to https://smartpm.pages.dev/
  3. Type your Username in the first box
  4. Type your Password in the second box
  5. Click the Sign In button
  6. You will see the Dashboard — this is your home screen
Don't have a username or password? Ask your Admin to create one for you.

3. Importing from SmartPlans

What is this?

SmartPlans is the app where your estimate is created. You can bring that estimate into SmartPM. This fills in your project with all the costs, rooms, tasks, and questions from the bid.

How to Import

  1. Go to the Dashboard (home screen)
  2. Click the "Import from SmartPlans" button
  3. Click "Choose JSON File" and pick your SmartPlans file
  4. Look at the preview — it shows what will be imported
  5. Click "Import Project"
  6. Done! Your project is now created with all the data

What Gets Imported

From SmartPlansGoes Into SmartPM
Project name and locationProject details
Contract valueFinancial summary
Item costs (material + labor)Schedule of Values (SOV)
MDF, IDF, and TR roomsInfrastructure locations
Equipment per roomEquipment list for each location
Cable runsCable records with budgeted footage
Phases and tasksWork Breakdown Structure (WBS)
Questions from the estimateRFIs
Budget numbers from SmartPlans are locked. Only Admins and Ops Managers can change them. PMs can only update what was actually installed.

4. Project Overview

What is this?

When you open a project, you see the Overview page first. It gives you a quick look at how the project is doing.

What You See

SectionWhat It Shows
Financial SummaryOriginal contract, current contract, total billed, and remaining balance
Project DetailsClient name, GC, location, and disciplines
Key DatesBid date, start date, and completion dates
AI Intelligence HubQuick buttons for the 6 AI tools

What the Numbers Mean

  • Original Contract — The starting contract amount
  • Current Contract — The contract amount after change orders
  • Total Billed — How much you have billed so far
  • Remaining Balance — How much is left to bill

Sidebar Menu

The sidebar on the left lists all the project sections. Click any item to go to that section. The sidebar includes: Overview, WBS, Infrastructure, SOV, Progress Billing, Change Orders, RFIs, Submittals, Daily Log, Punch List, Contacts, Documents, and AI Assistant.

5. Schedule of Values (SOV)

What is this?

The SOV is a list of every item in your project and its cost. Each row shows one item. It tells you the scheduled value, material cost, labor cost, and how much is done.

SOV Columns Explained

ColumnWhat It Means
Item #The line item number
DescriptionWhat the item is (like "Cat6 Cable" or "Patch Panels")
Scheduled ValueThe total dollar amount for this item
Material CostHow much the materials cost
Labor CostHow much the labor costs
% CompleteHow much of this item is done (0% to 100%)

How to Update the Percent Complete

  1. Click the SOV tab in the sidebar
  2. Find the item you want to update
  3. Click the Edit button on that row
  4. Change the % Complete number
  5. Click Save
Use the AI SOV Validation tool to check if your percent complete numbers match what the daily logs say.

6. Progress Billing

What is this?

Progress Billing is how you bill for work done each month. You create a billing period. Then you record how much work was done on each SOV item. The system calculates how much money is owed.

How to Bill Each Month

  1. Click Progress Billing in the sidebar
  2. Click "New Billing Period"
  3. Enter the billing period dates (start and end)
  4. For each SOV item, enter the work completed this period
  5. The system calculates retainage and payment due
  6. Click Save

G702 Summary

The G702 is a standard billing summary form. SmartPM creates this for you. It shows the original contract, change orders, total completed, retainage, and the amount due this period.

Retainage is a percentage of each payment that is held back until the project is done. It protects the owner.

7. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)

What is this?

The WBS shows all the work on your project. It is organized by Phase, then Location, then Task. Think of it like a tree: the phase is the trunk, locations are branches, and tasks are leaves.

Construction Phases

PhaseWhat Happens
Rough-InRunning cables, mounting backboards, installing pathways
Trim-OutInstalling faceplates, patch panels, and devices
Termination & TestConnecting cables and testing every run
CloseoutFinal cleanup, labeling, and as-built drawings

Use the filter buttons at the top to show only one phase at a time.

How to Update a Task

  1. Click WBS in the sidebar
  2. Find the task you want to update
  3. Click the Edit button on that task
  4. Enter how many units were installed (like 500 ft of cable)
  5. Enter how many labor hours were used
  6. Move the Progress slider to show how done it is
  7. Pick a Status: Not Started, In Progress, On Hold, or Complete
  8. Click Save

WBS Dashboard Numbers

  • Project Health — Green means on track. Yellow means watch out. Red means over budget.
  • Total Tasks — How many tasks are in the project
  • Overall Progress — A percentage showing how done the project is
  • Material Budget — Actual cost vs. budgeted cost
  • Labor Budget — Actual hours vs. budgeted hours
You only need to enter quantities and hours. SmartPM does the math for you. You do NOT need to enter dollar amounts.

8. Infrastructure

What is this?

The Infrastructure section tracks your telecom rooms. These are rooms like the MDF (Main Distribution Frame), IDF (Intermediate Distribution Frame), and TR (Telecom Room). Each room has equipment, cable runs, and labor.

What Each Tab Shows

TabWhat It Tracks
EquipmentPatch panels, switches, UPS units, racks — how many were budgeted vs. installed
Cable RunsCat6, fiber, coax cable — budgeted feet vs. installed feet
LaborWork hours — budgeted hours vs. actual hours

Each room shows a health indicator. Green means on budget. Yellow means getting close. Red means over budget.

Rooms are auto-created when you import from SmartPlans. You can also add rooms by hand.

9. Change Orders

What is this?

A change order is when the scope of work changes after the contract was signed. Maybe the client wants more cable runs. Maybe a room was removed. A change order tracks what changed, how much it costs, and how it affects the schedule.

How to Add a Change Order

  1. Click Change Orders in the sidebar
  2. Click "+ New Change Order"
  3. Pick the type: Addition, Deduction, or No-Cost Change
  4. Enter a description of what changed
  5. Enter the cost amount (positive or negative)
  6. Enter how many days it adds to the schedule (if any)
  7. Enter a reason for the change
  8. Click Save

Change orders start as Pending. They can be Approved or Rejected. Approved change orders update the current contract value.

Use the AI Impact Analysis tool to see how all your change orders affect the project budget and schedule.

10. RFIs (Requests for Information)

What is this?

An RFI is a question you need answered before you can do the work. For example: "What color should the faceplates be?" or "Which pathway should the cable follow?" RFIs are sent to the engineer or architect for an answer.

When you import from SmartPlans, any questions from the estimate are added as RFIs automatically.

RFI Status

StatusWhat It Means
OpenThe question has been submitted but not answered yet
AnsweredAn answer was received
ClosedThe RFI is done and no more action is needed
Use the AI Smart RFIs tool to find things in your project that need clarification. The AI writes the questions for you.

11. Submittals

What is this?

A submittal is product data you send to the architect or engineer for approval. For example, you might send a data sheet for a patch panel. They review it and say "Approved" or "Rejected."

Submittal Status

StatusWhat It Means
PendingNot yet sent out for review
SubmittedSent to the reviewer and waiting for a response
ApprovedThe product is approved for use
RejectedThe product is not approved — pick a different one
Revise & ResubmitFix the issues and send it again

12. Daily Log

What is this?

The Daily Log is where you write down what happened on the job site each day. This is very important. It is your record of the work.

What to Write Down

  • Weather — Was it sunny, rainy, or cold?
  • Crew size — How many workers were there?
  • Hours worked — How long did the crew work?
  • Work done — What did the crew do today?
  • Delays — Was anything late or stopped?
  • Materials — What materials were received or installed?
  • Visitors — Did anyone visit the site?
  • Safety — Were there any safety issues?
Be specific! Write room numbers, equipment names, and cable counts. The AI tools use your daily log entries to make reports.

13. Punch List

What is this?

The Punch List is a list of things that need to be fixed before the project is done. During a walkthrough, someone finds a problem (like a missing label or a loose cable). That problem goes on the Punch List.

Punch List Status

StatusWhat It Means
OpenThe problem was found but not fixed yet
In ProgressSomeone is working on fixing it
CompletedThe fix is done
VerifiedSomeone checked and confirmed the fix is good

Each punch item tracks the location, who is responsible, the due date, and any notes about the fix.

Use the AI Priority Plan tool to sort your punch items by importance. Safety items come first, then code issues, then functional problems, then cosmetic items.

14. Contacts

What is this?

The Contacts section is your project phone book. It stores the names, companies, phone numbers, and emails of everyone on the project.

How to Add a Contact

  1. Click Contacts in the sidebar
  2. Click "+ New Contact"
  3. Enter the person's name, company, role, phone, and email
  4. Click Save

You can edit or delete contacts at any time.

15. Documents

What is this?

The Documents section is where you upload and organize your project files. This includes specs, drawings, photos, and any other files you need.

How to Upload a File

  1. Click Documents in the sidebar
  2. Click the "Upload" button (or drag and drop a file)
  3. Pick a category tag for the file
  4. The file is uploaded and stored in the cloud

You can filter files by category. You can download any file. You can delete files you no longer need.

All documents are stored in the cloud. You can access them from any device.

17. Settings

What is this?

The Settings page lets you change project details. Only Admins and Ops Managers can see this page.

What You Can Change

  • Project name and project number
  • Client name and general contractor
  • Location and jurisdiction
  • Key dates — bid date, award date, start date, completion dates
  • Retainage percentage
  • Disciplines — the types of work on the project
  • Project status — Active, Punch List, Complete, or On Hold
If you need to change user passwords, Admins can do that from the header menu. Ops Managers can only change the PM password.

18. Re-importing from SmartPlans

What is this?

Sometimes the estimate in SmartPlans gets updated after you already imported it. You can re-import to get the new budget numbers. Your field data (like hours worked and quantities installed) will NOT be erased.

How to Re-import

  1. Open your project and go to the Overview tab
  2. Click the "Re-import from SmartPlans" button
  3. Pick the updated JSON file from SmartPlans
  4. SmartPM updates the budget numbers but keeps your field data
The Re-import button only shows on projects that were imported from SmartPlans. If you created the project by hand, this button will not appear.

19. Troubleshooting

Having a problem? Check the table below for help.

ProblemWhat to Do
I can't see my projectsYou can only see projects you created. Ask your Admin if you need access to other projects.
Import is not workingMake sure you are using a file from SmartPlans. It must be a JSON file. Check for error messages on the screen.
I don't see the Re-import buttonThis button only shows for projects that came from SmartPlans. Projects made by hand don't have it.
I can't log inCheck your username and password. Passwords are case-sensitive (capital letters matter). Ask your Admin for help.
I got a rate limit errorWait 60 seconds and try again. AI tools and imports have stricter limits.
I can't change the budget numbersBudget numbers from SmartPlans are locked. Only Admins and Ops Managers can change them.
The AI tool gave an errorThe AI needs data to work. Add SOV items, daily logs, or other data first. Empty projects don't work.
Email report won't sendThe email button opens your email app. If you don't have one, use "Copy to Clipboard" and paste into your email.
My changes won't saveCheck your internet connection. SmartPM needs the internet to save. You will see a banner if you are offline.
I can't delete a projectOnly Admins can delete projects. Ask your Admin to do this.
Still stuck? Contact your Admin or the 3D Technology Services support team for help.
